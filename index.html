ℹ️ INFO
Critical notes for reading market risk layers
1. Vesting Unlocks — Time-Triggered Pressure
Critical Warning: Even when circulating supply looks low, unlock events can flood the market with sell pressure. Not knowing the vesting schedule is like standing in front of a storm you did not see coming.
Analysis: Early investors often take profits on unlock days, which can create sharp price spikes and sudden drops. Always check when insiders can exit.
2. Maximum Supply and Inflation Dynamics
Critical Warning: Unlimited supply carries a permanent dilution risk. If there is no hard cap, every newly created coin reduces your share of the network.
Realistic View: Major projects such as Ethereum do not rely on a hard cap, but strong utility and burn mechanics can offset that pressure. For uncapped assets, what matters most is necessity and burn rate.
3. Liquidity and Slippage Risk
Critical Warning: Unrealized profit on the screen is not cash in your pocket. In thin markets, sell orders can push price down quickly, and exiting a weak book can cost more than entering it.
4. Market Cap Illusion
Critical Warning: A low price does not make a coin cheap, and a high price does not make it expensive. Real size is price multiplied by supply.
Note: Expecting 100x from an already bloated market cap is usually a high-risk bet against the odds.
5. Wallet Concentration and Manipulation
Critical Warning: If most of the supply is held by a few wallets, one whale can break the entire chart with a single sell. Whoever controls the supply often controls the direction.
6. Golden Rule: FOMO and Signal Traps
Critical Warning: When everyone praises the same coin while price is climbing vertically, someone is usually looking for exit liquidity. The biggest losses happen when you chase speed instead of probability.
